I have made a major shift in my coding approach, follow along to see why. 

### Why I'm Choosing to Understand the Machine

For most of my career, I've been building software at a high level. 

Modern frameworks. Clean abstractions. Fast iteration. 

Honestly—it worked... Until it didn't.

I could build and ship features quickly. Build interfaces. Solve problems. 
From the outside, everything seemed to be working. 

But underneath, and inside me, something felt... incomplete. 

### The Problem with "Just Making It Work"

At some point, I realized I didn't actually understand what my code was _doing_. 

Not really anyway.

I knew how to use tools—but not how they worked. 
I could build systems—but not explain how they worked.
I could kind of debug—but was very reliant on the UI and other tools to help me figure things out.

It really started to bother me that I didn't truly understand what I was doing. I was
in some ways "slinging code". I even felt like I stopped thinking about what I was doing at times. 

This really bothered me. 

I was drowning and didn't really even know it. 

### Slowing Down to Go Deeper

It was about this time that my company had me switch projects to C. 

I was told by some that "C is too hard", "I don't know why they would put you on that project".

I almost believed them. Until I started to write the code and read the code. 

C is the language that developed my coding skills. 

No safety nets. 
No hidden abstractions.
No magic. 

Memory, pointers, and the machine. 

Don't get me wrong, it **was** and **is** hard. But I have been making _daily_
gains and progress in my understanding of the machine.

I truly started learning what it means to be a developer and engineer. 

### What I am Chasing

I'm working on building a deeper mental model of how software works and how coding 
at its roots works.

- How memory is laid out
- How data moves through a system
- What really happens when a program runs 
- Why performance behaves the way it does 
- And many other things

Because I believe that understanding these things changes how you write and think 
about software. 

Even at higher levels.

### What this Blog is About

This blog is about documenting and continuing the journey. 

Not as an expert—but as someone actively figuring things out. 

You'll find:

- Breakdown of concepts I'm learning 
- Experiments and small projects
- Lessons from mistakes (there will be plenty)
- Attempts to explain complex systems simply

The goal is to make things clear—not complicated. 

#### The Calm Developer 

I believe good software isn't just about features or speed. 

It's about clarity.

It's about writing code that you—and others—can understand later. It's about building
systems that don't fight you.

And it's about staying calm in the process of learning and building. 

If you're interested or on a similar path, you are in the right place.
